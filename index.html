Brief Bio

I received my Ph.D. degree from Université Pierre et Marie Curie, Paris, France, working with Prof. Bruce Denby and Prof. Gerard Dreyfus (IEEE Fellow). My research focuses on multimodal machine learning, software engineering, and the applications of machine learning for audio and speech processing.

In addition to academic pursuits, I actively participate in competitive research, having achieved top ranks in data mining and machine learning competitions, including the ACM KDD Cup, Kaggle, Tianchi, and CCF BDCI. I am also a proud Kaggle Grandmaster.

Currently, I serve as an Associate Professor at the State Key Laboratory of Complex & Critical Software Environment at NUDT, China.

Research Interests

  • Computer Audition: Developing advanced models for sound recognition and audio signal processing.
  • Multi-modal Machine Learning: Fusing information from text, images, and audio for comprehensive AI solutions.
  • AI for Science: Applying AI techniques in scientific discovery, particularly in acoustic and biomedical domains.
  • Software Engineering: Enhancing development workflows with automation and AI integration.
